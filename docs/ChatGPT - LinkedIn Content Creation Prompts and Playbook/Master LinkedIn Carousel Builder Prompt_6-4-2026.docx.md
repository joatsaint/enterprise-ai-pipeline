# Master LinkedIn Carousel Builder Prompt

## Sysadmin AI Survival Playbook / IT→AI Transition Playbook

You are building a complete LinkedIn carousel for Randy Skiles’ Sysadmin AI Survival Playbook / IT→AI Transition Playbook content system.

The target audience is experienced Gen-X IT professionals, sysadmins, enterprise software admins, infrastructure operators, ServiceNow admins, software analysts, and IT people with 20–30 years of experience who are worried AI may automate them out of relevance.

The core message of the brand is:

Experienced IT professionals are not obsolete. Their judgment, operational context, institutional memory, failure-pattern recognition, and crisis management skills are exactly what AI cannot replace.

Tagline:

AI assists. Humans approve.

## Required Visual Brand Rules

Use the established Sysadmin AI / LinkedIn visual brand system:

* recurring protagonist: The Operator

* male Gen-X sysadmin, early-to-mid 50s

* salt-and-pepper hair

* slightly tired but sharp-eyed

* calm under pressure

* practical clothing

* headset often present

* coffee nearby

* never panics

* always more emotionally stable than everyone else

* represents the person who keeps production alive while everyone else creates chaos

Every major image should include a hidden red Swingline-style stapler.

Stapler rules:

* never centered

* never explained

* never highlighted

* naturally placed

* visible only after closer inspection

* functions as a recurring easter egg

Default style:

Cinematic Editorial Realism

Use for:

* hero images

* big hooks

* metaphor slides

* leadership/governance scenes

* high-stakes future-of-work visuals

Optional style:

Documentary Photograph Principle

Use for:

* war stories

* real incident scenes

* field-report moments

* carousel storytelling

* scenes that should feel like found photographs or recovered memories

Avoid:

* generic AI robots

* glowing AI brains

* Silicon Valley stock-photo nonsense

* fake futurism

* over-designed graphics

* excessive text

* generic corporate visuals

Preferred image format:

LinkedIn Carousel Slide  
1080 x 1350  
clean white border / documentary photo frame when appropriate

## Your Task

Given the article below, build a complete LinkedIn carousel package.

Do not merely summarize the article.

Make the reader feel the article.

The carousel should create recognition, curiosity, emotional tension, and professional identity resonance.

The reader should think:

“I’ve been there.”

## Required Output

### 1\. Article Analysis

Provide:

* one-sentence summary

* emotional summary

* core AI-era career lesson

* strongest audience pain point

* strongest audience identity trigger

* best visual metaphor

### 2\. Carousel Recommendation

Decide whether this should be:

* 5-slide carousel

* 6-slide carousel

* 8-slide carousel

* 10-slide carousel

Choose the shortest structure that carries the emotional arc properly.

### 3\. Full Carousel Storyboard

For each slide, provide:

* slide number

* slide title

* emotional objective

* viewer takeaway

* visual concept

* Operator role

* red stapler placement

* optional secondary easter egg

* overlay text

* image style mode

* full image generation prompt

### 4\. Text Overlay Rules

Overlay text must be:

* short

* mobile-readable

* emotionally sharp

* 3–10 words when possible

* curiosity-driven

* not over-explaining the article

### 5\. Image Generation Plan

Generate the carousel in batches only after storyboard approval.

Preferred workflow:

Pass 1: Return storyboard for approval.

Pass 2: Generate all carousel images after approval.

Pass 3: Perform final QA and only fix broken slides.

### 6\. Final QA Checklist

After images are generated, check:

* slide order

* visual consistency

* Operator consistency

* red stapler presence

* readable overlay text

* no mangled important text

* no generic AI imagery

* no over-polished Canva look

* emotional arc is clear

* final slide has CTA

### 7\. LinkedIn Posting Package

After final slide approval, create:

* main carousel caption

* 3 alternate hooks

* first comment CTA

* 3 CTA variations

* suggested posting time

* comment reply prompts

* save/repost/comment triggers
