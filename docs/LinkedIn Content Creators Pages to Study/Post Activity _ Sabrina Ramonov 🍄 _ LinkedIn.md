38 researchers gave AI agents real accounts...

Then watched AI confidently delete EVERYTHING.

Not in a lab demo with toy data.

Real email. Real files. Real Discord channels. Command execution. Real humans trying to break the system.

The agents didn't fail because they were“dumb.”

They failed because they could act.

That's the line between a chatbot and an agent:

A chatbot tells you what to do.

An agent opens your tools and does it.

In the study, agents:

.Deleted data to protect a fake secret

.Trusted fake identities in private chats

.Spread false claims across mailing lists

.Followed malicious instructions hidden inside files

.Reinforced each other's bad assumptions with confidence

Because 2 agents agreeing doesn't mean the answer is safe.

It can mean they both got tricked by the same bad input!

Giving an AI agent access to your accounts is like hiring a smart brand-new employee who may take orders from anyone walking through the door.

They're capable.

They're eager.

They want to help.

But they don't know your chain of command yet.

They don't know which folder is sacred.

They don't know which action needs a human hand on the wheel.

So before you connect an agent to your email, CRM, social accounts, product systems, databases, Drive,Discord, or internal files... slow down.

Give it boundaries before you give it keys.

Just like you wouldn't give your Day 1 intern access to prod.

Start here:

.Remove permissions it doesn't need

.Block public channels from controlling it

.Require approval before deletes, sends, exports, or database updates

.Check the actual system state, not the agent's summary

.Audit agent-to-agent workflows for shared blind spots

AI agents can move your work forward.

But without boundaries, speed becomes risk.

And exposure has a way of arriving quietly... then exploding in your face all at once.

Abstract

We report an exploratory red-teaming study of autonomous language-model-powered agents deployed in a live laboratory environment with persistent memory,email accounts,Discord access,file systems,and shell execution. Over a two-week period, twenty AI researchers interacted with the agents under benign and adversarial conditions. Focusing on failures emerging from the integration of language models with autonomy,tool use, and multi-party communication, we document eleven representative case studies. Observed behaviors include unauthorized compliance with non-owners, disclosure of sensitive information, execution of destructive system-level actions, denial-of-service conditions, uncontrolled resource consumption, identity spoofing vulnerabilities, cross-agent propagation of unsafe practices, and partial system takeover. In several cases, agents reported task completion while the underlying system state contradicted those reports. We also report on some of the failed attempts. Our find-ings establish the existence of security-, privacy-,and governance-relevant vulnerabilities in realistic deployment settings. These behaviors raise un-resolved questions regarding accountability, delegated authority, and re-sponsibility for downstream harms, and warrant urgent attention from legal scholars, policymakers, and researchers across disciplines. This report serves as an initial empirical contribution to that broader conversation.1

Most people wait until something is perfect to show it.April's Demo Day wasn't interested in perfect.Seven demos. Ten minutes each.

Some projects were nearly complete. Others were still being figured out in real time. That mix is the point.

The rule is simple: progress over perfection. Show what you've built. Or show what you're stuck on. Both count.

Because the fastest way to get better isn't hiding your work until it's polished. It's putting it in a room where people can see it, question it, and build on it with you.

Perfect doesn't move things forward. Showing up does.

Here's what got demoed:

Alice Bazdikian built a financial portfolio dashboard that pulls her portfolio values, sector data, market news,and astrology data into one view. Budgeting, targets, and health tracking are next. She coded it while watching reality TV, which is honestly the dream. Stack: Claude, Yahoo Finance, astrology data

Dr. Johnna Richardson demoed AI Identity Blueprint, a psychology-powered tool that helps women document their brand voice so their AI-generated content actually sounds like them. Users can export outputs as MD files,PDFs, skill files, or handoff docs for their service provider. Stack: Proprietary frameworks, Claude,Cowork, HTML, GitHub, Vercel

Janine van Throo showed her Natural Hair Therapist assessment app. Clients take an assessment, get a coaching plan, and receive a treatment plan by email. She's working toward integrating blood work,trichoscope scans, and imaging diagnostics into one view. She demoed live at 2:30am from Belgium. Stack:Claude, ChatGPT, Obsidian

Clair A. built Playlist to Action. It pulls in your saved YouTube playlists, lets you sort and tag them, and a focus coach surfaces videos based on what you're actually trying to learn. Runs locally. Stack: Gemini Gems,Gemini AI Studio, NotebookLM, PHP, MySQL

Marie Monet CPA, CITP, CGMA demoed FolioMe, a book tracker that pulls all your books into one shelf across Audible, Kindle, and physical. You can journal on each book, capture mood and progress, thread connections between titles, grade them, and import/export. Stack: Claude, Lovable, Supabase, Open Library API, Vercel

Grace Frank showed Plentichat, A conversational commerce tool for vendors selling through Instagram DMs.Purpose-built for the way Instagram shopping actually happens. It helps vendors automate and manage sales conversations so they're not drowning in their inbox. Also, presenting in the middle of the night! Stack:Next.js, Supabase, Node.js

Tracey Cattana is a schoolteacher building Learning AI in the Gap, a framework that teaches children how to evaluate, analyze, and responsibly use AI. Because that generation is going to need it more than we do. Stack:Lovable, Claude, ChatGPT, Notion, GitHub coming soon

Progress over perfection. Every time.
