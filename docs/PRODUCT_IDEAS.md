## IDEA 002 — Resume Crossmatch AI Prompt Tool

**Status:** Idea captured — build after first paid sale
**Estimated price point:** $17-$27 (standalone prompt pack + instructions)
**Target buyer:** IT professionals evaluating their AI readiness

**Concept:**
A structured prompt that takes a user's resume (or job history summary) and
crossmatches it against a defined AI skills framework to identify:
- Current transferable strengths (what they already have)
- Critical gaps (what's missing for target roles)
- Suggested learning path with specific resources
- Realistic timeline estimate for each gap

**Why it's valuable:**
IT professionals know they need to pivot but don't know where they stand.
A resume gap analysis gives them a concrete starting point and a roadmap.
This is the "where am I now" tool — the lead magnet covers "where should I go."

**Prompt design principles:**
- User pastes their resume or describes their background in plain text
- No proprietary data required — job titles and skill descriptions only
- Output: structured gap analysis with three sections:
  1. Strengths to leverage (with specific AI role mappings)
  2. Gaps to close (ranked by hiring frequency in job postings)
  3. 90-day learning path (specific courses, projects, certifications)
- Works in any LLM — ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini

**Packaging options:**
- PDF prompt pack with instructions ($17)
- PDF prompt pack + video walkthrough ($27)
- Bundle with lead magnet deep dive series ($37)

**Production notes:**
- Validate prompt against 3-5 real IT professional resumes before selling
- Randy's own resume is the first test case
- Pain point report confirms this is a top audience desire

## IDEA 003 — Executive AI Integration Framework Prompt

**Status:** Idea captured — build after Resume Crossmatch tool is validated
**Estimated price point:** $47-$97 (premium prompt framework)
**Target buyer:** IT directors, VPs, and managers evaluating AI adoption

**Concept:**
A structured prompt series that guides executives through AI adoption
decision-making without requiring them to input proprietary data.
Uses generic operational descriptions to produce:
- Actionable integration guidelines specific to their situation
- Tool and methodology recommendations with rationale
- Risk and gotcha warnings for common AI adoption mistakes
- Safety and governance checklist for AI decisions

**Why it's valuable:**
Executives want to adopt AI but are overwhelmed by vendor marketing and
afraid of making expensive mistakes. A framework that asks the right
questions and outputs actionable guidance — without requiring them to
expose sensitive business data — removes the two biggest barriers.

**Prompt design (question series approach):**
The prompt asks a series of generic questions one at a time:
1. What operational area are you trying to improve? (e.g., "customer support",
   "data reporting", "content creation" — no specifics needed)
2. What is the current manual process? (described generically)
3. What does success look like in 90 days? (outcome, not metrics)
4. What are your team's current technical capabilities? (scale of 1-5)
5. What compliance or security constraints exist? (industry category only)

Output sections:
- Recommended approach (with reasoning)
- Tool options at three budget tiers (free, mid, enterprise)
- Implementation sequence (what to do first, second, third)
- Gotchas to watch for (the things vendors don't tell you)
- Safety and governance checklist
- When to bring in outside help vs. build internally

**Packaging options:**
- Prompt framework PDF with instructions ($47)
- Prompt framework + workshop facilitation guide ($97)
- White-label version for consultants ($197)

**Production notes:**
- This is a different buyer persona than the career-pivot audience
- May require a separate landing page and LinkedIn targeting
- Validate with 2-3 IT directors before pricing
- Randy's 25 years of IT experience is the credibility signal here
