## Ideal Customer Profile

**Who they are:**
Capable people who got left behind by the speed of change and are quietly
terrified of becoming irrelevant.

Employed professionals 35-55 with 10-25 years of experience in IT, operations,
finance, project management, or enterprise administration. They are competent —
often the most experienced person in the room — but overwhelmed by the pace of
AI adoption and uncertain whether their skills will still matter in 3 years.

**They are NOT:**
- Job seekers looking for an AI role (that's a different audience)
- Recent CS graduates learning to code
- Entrepreneurs building AI products

**Specific IT sub-segment (Randy's credibility anchor):**
System admins, ITSM specialists, ServiceNow admins, enterprise IT professionals.
Randy's 25-year IT background gives him direct credibility with this group.

**What they feel:**
Not left behind yet — but they can feel it coming. They watch AI announcements
with a mix of curiosity and dread. They're not sure whether to learn it, ignore
it, or quietly panic. They have something to lose — income, status, relevance —
and that makes them motivated buyers.

**What they want:**
To stay indispensable. Not to find a new job. To keep the one they have and
make it more secure. To stop feeling like they're the last person in the room
who hasn't figured this out.

**Demographics:**
- Age: 35-55
- Income: $90K-$140K median — can afford $27-$97 without approval
- Platform: LinkedIn primary, YouTube secondary
- Psychological state: Competent but uncertain. Experienced but intimidated.

**The one sentence that captures them:**
*"Capable people who got left behind by the speed of change and are quietly
terrified of becoming irrelevant."*
