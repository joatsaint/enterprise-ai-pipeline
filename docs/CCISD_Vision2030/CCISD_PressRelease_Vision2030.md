Clear Creek ISD Launches Vision 2030 Plan

League City, Texas- The Clear Creek Independent School District is pleased to announce strategic initiatives that will allow students to pursue their passions in ways that do not exist today. The Board of Trustees unanimously approved the Vision 2030 Plan which outlines bold objectives in support of student learning such as access to full-day prekindergarten for all and reimagining the intermediate school experience.

“Our journey began with defining what characteristics and skills do we want students to learn, and we began with our current fifth grade students as they will be graduating in 2030," said Dr. Karen Engle, Superintendent of Schools.“We always put kids first, so this was a natural starting point. We asked parents, teachers, board members, business leaders for input and they helped create the Profile of a Learner."

The profile of a learner articulates eight competencies, or what the district now refers to as the 8Cs of CCISD.“When I talk with business leaders and ask them what you want your future workforce to be and they always refer to power skills such as being a collaborator, a creator, a critical thinker, confident and compassionate."

The Vision 2030 team used this profile and began strategic planning in January. The team did their work at the 1958 building at NASA Johnson Space Center, a hub of exciting opportunities for the future of space exploration.

The Vision team set out the following goals to be achieved by 2030.

.Clear Creek ISD will offer pre-kindergarten to all District families in search of a quality early education.

Clear Creek ISD will be a Leader in Me District.

.All Clear Creek ISD intermediate schools will be nationally recognized as centers of innovation,creation, and collaboration.

.All Clear Creek ISD graduates will be creators, critical thinkers, collaborators, contributors,communicators, connectors, compassionate and confident.

For more information on the Vision 2030 plan, visit www.ccisd.net/vision2030.
