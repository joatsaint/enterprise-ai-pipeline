# LinkedIn Group Post Hooks — Swappable Bank

**Purpose:** Never post a bare link to a LinkedIn group again. Lead with a 1-2 line hook,
then the link below it. Rotate hooks so posting to 20 groups doesn't look like a spam blast
(vary the first line per group). Groups reward *discussion* — when a group is active, use a
hook that ends in a question.

**Rules of thumb**
- 1-2 lines max, then the article link on its own line below.
- Vary the hook per group. Never paste the identical post to all 20 (spam-flag risk).
- For active/discussion groups, prefer a question-ending hook (pulls comments → surfaces the post).
- For quiet/link-only groups, a tight curiosity hook is fine.
- Respect each group's posting rules.

---

## Ready-to-use variants — "From Burned Out to Fired UP!" (Featured article)

**1. The Confession (vulnerable)**
> Three years ago I was so burned out I'd sworn I'd never learn anything new in IT again. 25 years in, one foot out the door. This is the story of what changed my mind 👇

**2. The Irony (curiosity)**
> Three years ago I almost left IT to go work with animals. Then AI made me fall back in love with infrastructure. Here's the whole story 👇

**3. The Question (discussion bait)**
> Fellow IT vets — anyone else hit the point where you were so burned out you thought about leaving tech entirely? That was me. Here's what pulled me back 👇 Curious if this resonates.

**4. The Contrarian (challenge the premise)**
> "Will AI replace me?" is the wrong question entirely. After 25 years in IT, here's the question I started asking instead — and why it changed my career 👇

**5. The Specific Detail (concrete)**
> I had 200-300 YouTube transcripts scattered across ChatGPT, Gemini, and Canva and couldn't find anything. Trying to fix that one annoyance turned into the best career move I've made 👇

**6. The Peer Call-out + Outcome**
> If you've spent years in IT operations, you already know how to work with AI — you've just never pointed your infrastructure questions at it. Here's what happened when I did 👇

**7. The Pattern / Timing**
> In the early 2000s the question was "do you know Excel?" Right now nobody's asking "do you know how to work with AI agents?" — but they will be. Here's how I got a head start 👇

**8. The Proof Tease**
> I built an AI system that runs my job without me — and it's the best career move I ever made. Not as an AI engineer. As an operator. Here's how 👇

## Reusable archetypes — build hooks for ANY future article

Use these formulas to spin 6-8 variants for the next article in minutes.

| Archetype | Formula | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| **The Confession** | Admit something most people won't say out loud → "here's the story" | Personal/narrative pieces |
| **The Irony** | Set up an expectation, then the unexpected twist | Strong opening anecdote |
| **The Question** | Ask the reader the exact pain they're living → invite them in | Active discussion groups |
| **The Contrarian** | "Everyone thinks X. It's actually Y." | Reframe / myth-busting articles |
| **The Specific Detail** | Lead with one concrete, believable number/scene | Build/how-it-happened pieces |
| **The Peer Call-out** | "Fellow [role] —" + a shared experience | Niche/identity groups |
| **The Pattern / Timing** | "Last time X happened, Y. It's happening again." | Trend / opportunity articles |
| **The Proof Tease** | State the result, withhold the how → "here's how" | Outcome-driven articles |

**Default rotation:** pick a different archetype for each group, lead question-style hooks
into your most active groups, and keep the link on its own line directly below the hook.
