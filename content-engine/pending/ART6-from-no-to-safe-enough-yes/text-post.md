The AI boom in the enterprise will not be limited by what the model can do.

It will be limited by what it's allowed to touch.

That's the part the hype keeps skipping. Everybody is asking "can the AI write the code?" That's the easy question. The hard questions are the ones experienced IT people ask reflexively:

What data is this touching?
Who approved it?
Can we roll it back?
Is there a log?
Did we scrub the PII?
Who owns the outcome when it's wrong at 2 AM?

That's not fear. That's operational judgment. And it's about to become more valuable, not less.

For years a lot of seasoned operators have assumed their skepticism makes them behind the curve. It might be the opposite. The person who can imagine the failure state isn't the blocker. They're the only one carrying the pager for the nightmare scenario.

The real opportunity isn't becoming an AI engineer. It's becoming the person who can move a company from "no AI touches anything" to "here is a safe, permissioned, auditable AI-assisted workflow."

That's not prompt engineering. That's systems judgment.

AI assists. Humans approve. Simple. Boring. Useful — the exact opposite of most vendor webinars.

If you've spent years connecting security, permissions, workflow, and production consequences, you're closer to this lane than you think.
