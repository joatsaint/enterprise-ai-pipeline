One thing I left implicit in the post: the ladder works because each rung builds trust before the next one. A company that lets AI summarize tickets cleanly for three months has earned the right to ask "can it draft responses now?" Skipping rungs is how you get an incident report with jazz hands.

The Lamar University account provisioning example is worth sitting with. Twenty people a day, thirty minutes each — obviously screaming for automation. Worth saying plainly: distrust comes in three flavors. Cynical distrust ("AI is hype"). Lazy distrust ("I survived Windows NT, leave me alone"). And useful distrust ("what happens if this is wrong and can we roll it back?"). Only the third one pays. The first two are just career risk wearing a costume.
