## NEWSLETTER

Subject: From No to Safe Enough Yes

Years ago at American National, anything touching the network had to flow through one senior security officer near the top of the company.

To a lot of people, he looked like a blocker. The guy who slowed everything down. The guy who wanted proof before letting anything near the network.

But his nightmare was simple: he didn't want to walk into the CEO's office and explain that the enterprise database had been encrypted, stolen, or made unrecoverable.

That's not paranoia. That's accountability.

I've been thinking about him a lot lately, because the AI era is about to make his kind of judgment more valuable than ever.

Here's the thing the hype misses. The AI boom in the enterprise will not be limited by what the models can do. It'll be limited by what they're allowed to touch. Should this tool see internal data? Who approves the workflow? Where's the audit trail? Can it modify production? Can it create accounts without a human in the loop?

That's the work. Not writing the Python script — the script is easy. The hard part is designing a workflow the organization can actually approve.

And the people best positioned to do that aren't 23-year-old founders in fresh hoodies. They're the sysadmins, ServiceNow admins, infrastructure operators, and security analysts who already understand permissions, change management, approval chains, and production consequences.

The opportunity isn't "become an AI engineer."

It's becoming the person who can say: "No, not there. Not that way. Not with that data. But here is a safer version we can actually approve."

That's how enterprises move. Not from no to magic. From no to safe enough yes.
